Types of grammar (0.5 hours)

↵ Back to module homepage

As discussed in an earlier task, grammar is not just about sentences, verb endings, and stuff like that. Grammar is every aspect of the system governing how language works. Let's take a moment to look at what different kinds of "grammar" there are in a language. This will also be useful because the kinds of grammar match very closely to the different subfields of linguistics (things like "syntax", "phonetics", etc.) and the modules of this subject.

Looking at the chapter you read in part 2 of this module, or at any other resources you can find, fill in the blanks below with the names of different subfields of linguistic study.

  • Descriptive grammar rules about how sounds work and go together are part of ____ and ____.
  • Descriptive grammar rules about how words are created are part of ____.
  • Descriptive grammar rules about how different words are put together to make a sentence are part of ____.
  • Descriptive grammar rules about how meanings are put together to express a more specific idea are part of ____.
  • Descriptive grammar rules about how speakers and context influence the meaning of language are part of ____.

When you're finished, you can click below to check your answers.

  • Descriptive grammar rules about how sounds work and go together are part of phonetics and phonology.
  • Descriptive grammar rules about how words are created are part of morphology.
  • Descriptive grammar rules about how different words are put together to make a sentence are part of syntax.
  • Descriptive grammar rules about how meanings are put together to express a more specific idea are part of semantics.
  • Descriptive grammar rules about how speakers and context influence the meaning of language are part of pragmatics.
