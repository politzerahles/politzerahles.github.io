Electrophysiology (8-12 hours)

↵ Back to class homepage

Module overview

The goal of this module is for you to get familiar with what electrophysiology is and how it can be used to address psycholinguistic questions. By the end of the module, hopefully you will be able to do the following things:

  • Explain how electrophysiology is different from other common neuroscience methods, and describe its advantages and disadvantages;
  • Demonstrate awareness of what ERP components do and do not represent;
  • Discuss and analyze the meaning of at least one ERP component, and explain how it can be used to study linguistic or psycholinguistic questions.

Module instructions

This module includes six tasks. Task 1 and task 3 are compulsory for this module. On the other hand, out of Tasks 2a-2d, you just need to do any one.

Module activities

  1. Intro to neurolinguistic methods (3 hours)
  2. Learning about ERP components
    1. The N400 (2 hours)
    2. The Lateralized Readiness Potential (2 hours)
    3. The Mismatch Negativity (2 hours)
    4. The N400 vs. the P600 (2 hours)
  3. How ERP components should and should not be understood (3 hours)
  4. Advanced level (4 hours)
